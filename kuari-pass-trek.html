Overview

The Kuari Pass Trek is a celebrated winter trekking experience in Uttarakhand, nestled in the Garhwal Himalayas. Also known as the Lord Curzon Trail, this trek offers a beautiful mix of heritage, panoramic Himalayan views, alpine meadows, and oak forests. Situated at an altitude of 12,516 ft, Kuari Pass opens a gateway to some of the most majestic Himalayan peaks.
This trek takes you through charming villages, deep woods, and vast grasslands like Gorson Bugyal, while offering spectacular views of Nanda Devi, Dronagiri, Chaukhamba, Hathi Ghoda, and Neelkanth peaks. It’s ideal for both beginners and seasoned trekkers looking to explore nature's grandeur in peaceful solitude.
Despite being a high-altitude trek, Kuari Pass is considered relatively easy to moderate in difficulty. The route is scenic and rich with culture and biodiversity, offering everything from pine forests to the chance to spot wildlife. Whether you’re walking under snow-laden trees or camping under a starlit sky, this trek promises an experience of serenity and adventure.

Itinerary

Well-paced itinerary with forest routes, ridge walks, and an iconic viewpoint that showcases majestic Himalayan ranges.

DAY 0 - Overnight Journey – Delhi to Rishikesh (240 km / 6–7 hrs)

  • Assemble at the designated boarding point in Delhi by evening (around 9–10 PM).
  • Meet your trek mates and Bighorn India team — intros, briefing, and cheerful vibes.
  • Begin your overnight journey to Rishikesh in a comfortable tempo traveler or bus.
  • Carry your essentials — power bank, light snacks, water bottle, neck pillow, etc.
  • The drive winds through NH 58, passing towns lit up at night.
  • Overnight travel filled with music, laughter, and excitement for the trek ahead.
  • Reach Rishikesh early morning (Day 1) for a fresh start and onward journey to Joshimath.

DAY 1 - Rishikesh to Joshimath (256 km | 10-11 hours | 6,725 ft)

  • Kickstart your Kuari Pass adventure with a scenic road journey from Rishikesh to Joshimath, the base camp for the trek.
  • Drive alongside the Ganga and Alaknanda rivers, crossing the sacred sangams of Devprayag, Rudraprayag, and Karnaprayag.
  • As you climb, the landscape transforms from bustling river valleys to serene mountain terrains and pine forests.
  • Settle into your guesthouse. Meet the Bighorn India team, get to know your trek mates, and attend a short briefing about the days ahead.
  • Relish hot local cuisine and breathe in the mountain air as you prepare for the trekking journey ahead.
  • Comfortable overnight accommodation at Joshimath.

Day 2: Gulling Top to Khullara Top

Continue trekking through dense forests of oak, pine, and rhododendron. The trail is moderately steep and scenic with glimpses of distant peaks. Reach Khullara Top, a picturesque high-altitude meadow, and set up camp for the night.

  • Distance: 6 km trek
  • Duration: 5–6 hours
  • Altitude: 11,075 ft

Day 3: Khullara Top to Kuari Pass Summit & Return to Khullara

Today is summit day. Trek uphill along a narrow ridge, with mesmerizing views on both sides. Reach Kuari Pass, your highest point, and soak in panoramic views of Nanda Devi, Kamet, Trishul, and more. After some time at the top, descend back to Khullara Top.

  • Distance: 12 km trek (round trip)
  • Duration:8–10 hours
  • Altitude:12,516 ft (Kuari Pass)

Day 4: Khullara Top to Dhak Village & Drive to Joshimath

Descend through the same forested trail back to Dhak Village. From here, you’ll drive back to Joshimath. Check in and rest after your final day of trekking.

  • Distance: 11 km trek + drive
  • Duration: 8–10 hours total
  • Altitude: 6,724 ft (Joshimath)

Day 5: Departure from Joshimath

After breakfast, bid farewell to the mountains and begin your journey home. It takes around 9–10 hours to reach Rishikesh/Haridwar by road.

Why Should You Do the Kuari Pass Trek?

  • Breathtaking Mountain Views: The Kuari Pass Trek is renowned for its wide-ranging views of Himalayan giants like Nanda Devi, Kamet, Chaukhamba, Trishul, and Hathi Ghoda Parbat, making it a visual feast throughout the journey.
  • Rich Cultural Experience:Trek through remote villages like Tugasi, Guling, and Khullara, experiencing traditional Uttarakhandi architecture and the warm hospitality of locals.
  • Forests & Meadows : Hike through stunning oak, walnut, and rhododendron forests, opening into vast alpine meadows like Gorson Bugyal, which remain snow-covered in winter.
  • Perfect for Stargazing:Clear winter skies make this trek a dream for stargazers. On cloudless nights, you might even spot the Milky Way from your campsite.
  • Ideal for Beginners: With a well-paced itinerary and relatively easy terrain, the Kuari Pass Trek is suitable for first-time trekkers with a decent level of fitness.

Best Time to Do Kuari Pass Trek

The winter months from November to March are considered the best time to do the Kuari Pass Trek. During this period, the trail is covered with snow, and skies remain clear, offering unmatched views and a true Himalayan winter trekking experience.

How Difficult is Kuari Pass Trek?

Kuari Pass Trek falls under the easy to moderate difficulty level. It involves a mix of gradual ascents and descents, with the summit day being slightly more demanding. Basic fitness and acclimatization are sufficient to complete the trek safely and enjoyably.

How to Reach Kuari Pass Trek?

Joshimath is the base camp for the Kuari Pass Trek. Here's how you can reach it:

  • By Air: The nearest airport is Jolly Grant Airport (Dehradun), around 270 km from Joshimath. From the airport, you can hire a cab or take a bus to reach Joshimath.
  • Rich Cultural Experience:Trek through remote villages like Tugasi, Guling, and Khullara, experiencing traditional Uttarakhandi architecture and the warm hospitality of locals.
  • By Train: The closest railway station is Rishikesh Railway Station. From Rishikesh, you can take a shared cab or private transport to Joshimath, which is around a 9–10-hour drive.
  • By Road: Regular buses are available from Rishikesh or Haridwar to Joshimath. It's recommended to start early in the morning to reach before nightfall.
